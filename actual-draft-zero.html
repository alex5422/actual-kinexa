Actual Draft Zero · April 2025
Founding document

What is actually
happening right now.

First draft · Open to disagreement · Subject to revision

Something is changing in the human being — biologically, cognitively, socially. Most thinking spaces are not looking at it directly.

The speed of change in medicine and artificial intelligence is outpacing our ability to think about it clearly. Not because we lack intelligence — because we lack the right kind of space. Somewhere quiet enough to think. Honest enough to say what we don't know. Small enough to stay real.

Actual starts from a specific vantage point: people who have worked with the human body at its most fragile have something to say about this moment that engineers and institutional experts do not. The individual becoming something new — biologically augmented, cognitively extended, medically transformed — is not an abstraction. It is a person in front of you.

What Actual refuses
×PDFs no one reads past page three
×Events where performance replaces thought
×Positions designed to offend no one
×Expertise disconnected from lived experience
×Consensus as a substitute for truth

Actual publishes short, signed notes. Positions, not syntheses. Questions asked plainly. Real disagreements between people who are not trying to agree — without mediation designed to smooth things over.

Beauty and calm are not decoration here. They are the condition for thought that doesn't just react. A slow space in a fast world is not a luxury. It is a method.

This document is deliberately unfinished. The questions in the panel beside it are part of the text. So are the responses it generates.

Open questions in this draft
01 Who gets to speak about the individual becoming — and on whose behalf? ›
Any space that thinks about "the human" risks speaking for people who aren't in the room. How does Actual avoid that — structurally, not just symbolically?
02 Can a space be both calm and honest about what's coming? ›
Calm is easy when things are going well. The question is whether Actual can hold that register when the subject matter is genuinely alarming — which it sometimes will be.
03 Medical AI will create massive access inequalities. Does Actual observe or take sides? ›
The most powerful diagnostic AI tools will reach well-funded health systems first. Actual has no answer yet on where the line between knowledge and advocacy sits.
04 How does this differ from Noema, or Works in Progress? ›
Noema thinks from the West Coast. Works in Progress thinks in solutions. Actual thinks from the body — from clinical experience, from what it means to care for someone. Is that enough to justify another space?
05 At what point does Actual become political — and can it avoid it? ›
Every subject Actual touches has a political reading available. Choosing what not to cover is itself a political act. This is not a problem to solve — it's a tension to manage consciously.
